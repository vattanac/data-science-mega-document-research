ABSTRACT (paraphrased):
-----------------------
This paper presents the first physical demonstration of Reynolds flocking
rules with real fixed-wing aircraft flying outdoors. The work adapts the
classic separation, alignment, and cohesion rules to handle fixed-wing
constraints: minimum airspeed, limited turn rate, no hovering, and wind
disturbances. Up to 5 autonomous aircraft flew in coordinated flocks,
exhibiting emergent behaviors including circular orbiting, dynamic leader
switching, and elastic deformation. The platform used SwingletCAM flying
wings with XBee radio communication at 5 Hz.

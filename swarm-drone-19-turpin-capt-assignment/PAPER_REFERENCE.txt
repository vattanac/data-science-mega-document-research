ABSTRACT (paraphrased):
-----------------------
This paper addresses the problem of assigning goals to robots and
planning collision-free trajectories simultaneously. The authors prove
a remarkable theorem: when robots are identical and move along
straight-line paths at proportional speeds, the optimal assignment
(minimizing total squared distance) is automatically collision-free.
This eliminates the need for separate collision avoidance planning.
The optimal assignment is computed using the Hungarian algorithm in
O(N³) time. The result extends to 2D and 3D environments and is
demonstrated on teams of quadrotor UAVs performing formation
transitions. This work is foundational for commercial drone show
choreography, warehouse robotics, and any multi-robot system
requiring collision-free reconfiguration.

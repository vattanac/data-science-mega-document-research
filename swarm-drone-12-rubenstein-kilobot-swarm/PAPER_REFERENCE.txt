ABSTRACT (paraphrased):
-----------------------
The paper demonstrates programmable self-assembly with 1,024 Kilobots —
simple robots that cost ~$14 each, communicate via infrared, and move
using vibration motors. Starting from an unordered group, the robots
collectively form complex predetermined 2D shapes (stars, wrenches,
letters) using only local sensing and communication with no centralized
control. The algorithm uses gradient-based localization, edge following,
and local shape-checking to achieve robust assembly despite significant
hardware noise and ~2-5% robot failure rate.

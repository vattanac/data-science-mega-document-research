ABSTRACT (paraphrased):
-----------------------
This paper applies game-theoretic analysis to adversarial
swarm-on-swarm combat engagements. The authors model the problem as
a two-player zero-sum game where an attacking swarm attempts to reach
defended targets while a defensive swarm attempts to intercept. The
framework analyzes optimal force allocation strategies using
Lanchester's attrition laws (Linear and Square), finding Nash
equilibria that represent optimal play for both sides. Key findings
include: concentration gives disproportionate advantage under the
Square Law, mixed (randomized) strategies are often optimal to
prevent exploitation, and information advantage (knowing enemy
allocation) is the single most decisive factor. The work provides
foundational theory for autonomous drone swarm tactics in contested
environments.

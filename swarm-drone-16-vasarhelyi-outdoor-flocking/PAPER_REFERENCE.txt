ABSTRACT (paraphrased):
-----------------------
This paper demonstrates optimized autonomous flocking with up to 30
drones in outdoor conditions, using only onboard GPS and local
communication — no external infrastructure. The key innovation is
evolutionary optimization of flocking parameters in a realistic
simulator that models GPS noise, communication delays, and physical
constraints. The optimized parameters transfer directly to real drones
without additional tuning, achieving zero collisions across all test
flights. The flocking model uses four local interaction rules
(repulsion, alignment, cohesion, self-propulsion) inspired by the
Vicsek model of collective motion. This represents the largest
outdoor autonomous drone flock demonstrated at the time of publication,
bridging statistical physics theory with practical swarm engineering.

ABSTRACT (paraphrased):
-----------------------
This paper describes a system for controlling swarms of agile micro
quadrotors weighing only 33 grams. The platform uses a two-layer
control architecture: a fast 500 Hz onboard attitude controller and
a 100 Hz off-board position controller using Vicon motion capture.
Minimum-snap polynomial trajectories are generated for smooth,
collision-free flight paths. The Hungarian algorithm provides optimal
assignment for formation transitions. The system demonstrated 20
drones flying simultaneously in tight 3D formations, performing
coordinated gap traversal maneuvers, and transitioning smoothly
between formation shapes — all with sub-centimeter position accuracy.
This work from UPenn's GRASP Lab became one of the most viral
robotics demonstrations in history, showcasing the potential of
nano-scale aerial robot swarms.

ABSTRACT (paraphrased):
-----------------------
This paper presents a method for collision avoidance in teams of micro
air vehicles (MAVs) using only on-board communication hardware for
relative localization. Instead of requiring dedicated ranging sensors
(UWB, cameras, infrared), the system extracts distance estimates from
RSSI (Received Signal Strength Indicator) measurements of the radio
signals already used for inter-drone communication. An Extended Kalman
Filter fuses noisy RSSI readings with a motion model to produce stable
range estimates. Despite RSSI's limited accuracy (~1-2m), the system
achieves 100% collision avoidance success across all test flights with
up to 4 drones. The approach adds zero weight and zero cost since it
leverages existing communication hardware, making it ideal for
resource-constrained swarms.
